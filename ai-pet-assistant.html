Food Safety

Is this food safe for my dog? Now you can check instantly.

The food safety checker is one of the most-used features in PetNudge. Here is how it works and why pet owners love it.

1

Open the food safety scanner

Tap the AI assistant in PetNudge and select the food safety camera option. The scanner activates your iPhone camera and is ready to identify food items in real time. It works in any lighting condition and can recognize hundreds of common foods.

2

Point your camera at the food

Hold your phone over the food you want to check. The AI identifies it within seconds. You can scan fresh fruits and vegetables, cooked dishes, snacks, packaged foods, and even items from your own plate. If the AI is not sure what it is looking at, it will ask you to describe the food in text instead.

3

Get your safety verdict

The result appears instantly with a clear safety rating. A green result means the food is safe for your pet. Yellow means caution is needed, typically in terms of quantity or preparation. Red means the food is toxic and should be kept away from your pet entirely. Each result includes a detailed explanation of why the food is safe or dangerous, what symptoms to watch for if your pet has already eaten it, and what to do next.

Common foods pet owners ask about

Toxic for Dogs

  • ✗ Chocolate (theobromine)
  • ✗ Grapes and raisins
  • ✗ Onions and garlic
  • ✗ Xylitol (artificial sweetener)
  • ✗ Macadamia nuts
  • ✗ Avocado (persin)

Use Caution

  • ⚠ Cheese (small amounts OK)
  • ⚠ Peanut butter (check for xylitol)
  • ⚠ Bread (plain, small amounts)
  • ⚠ Eggs (cooked only)
  • ⚠ Milk (many pets are lactose intolerant)
  • ⚠ Tomatoes (ripe fruit only)

Safe for Dogs

  • ✓ Carrots
  • ✓ Blueberries
  • ✓ Watermelon (seedless)
  • ✓ Cooked chicken (plain)
  • ✓ Sweet potato (cooked)
  • ✓ Apple slices (no seeds)

Symptom Analysis

Describe symptoms. Get structured guidance.

When your pet is not acting like themselves, the AI symptom checker helps you understand what might be happening and how to respond.

📝

Describe in plain language

You do not need medical terminology. Just describe what you observe. "My dog has been vomiting since this morning and seems tired." "My cat is scratching her ear constantly and shaking her head." "My puppy has not eaten in two days." The AI understands natural language and asks follow-up questions when more detail would help provide a better response.

📊

Receive structured analysis

The AI provides a clear breakdown: possible causes ranked by likelihood, recommended next steps, urgency level (monitor at home, schedule a vet visit, or seek emergency care), and what information to prepare for your vet. This structured format helps you make calm, informed decisions rather than panicking or dismissing something that needs attention.

🏥

Find nearby veterinarians

If the AI determines that your pet's symptoms warrant professional attention, PetNudge can show you nearby veterinary clinics and emergency animal hospitals. You get directions, phone numbers, and opening hours so you can act quickly when time matters. The nearby vet finder works with your location to surface the closest options.

Important Guidance

When to use the AI assistant vs. when to see the vet

The AI assistant is a powerful tool for everyday pet care questions, but it is not a substitute for professional veterinary care. Here is how to use it responsibly.

🤖

Great for the AI assistant

  • ✓ "Can my dog eat this food?"
  • ✓ "How much should I feed my 3-year-old Labrador?"
  • ✓ "Why does my cat knead blankets?"
  • ✓ "What are common allergies in French Bulldogs?"
  • ✓ "How often should I brush my dog's teeth?"
  • ✓ "Is it normal for puppies to sleep this much?"
  • ✓ "What supplements are good for senior cats?"
  • ✓ General nutrition and diet guidance
  • ✓ Training tips and behavioral questions
  • ✓ Grooming schedules and coat care
🏥

See your veterinarian

  • ❌ Persistent vomiting or diarrhea
  • ❌ Difficulty breathing or choking
  • ❌ Seizures or loss of consciousness
  • ❌ Ingestion of a known toxic substance
  • ❌ Bleeding that does not stop
  • ❌ Sudden inability to walk or stand
  • ❌ Eye injuries or sudden blindness
  • ❌ Refusing food and water for 24+ hours
  • ❌ Signs of extreme pain (whimpering, hiding)
  • ❌ Any situation that feels like an emergency

When in doubt, always err on the side of caution and consult your veterinarian. The AI assistant will always recommend professional care when symptoms suggest a potentially serious condition.
