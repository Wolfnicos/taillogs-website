What to Do When You Find a Lost Pet on the Street

PetNudge Team March 2, 2026 8 min read
Home/Blog/Found a Lost Pet

You're walking home from work, running errands, or simply enjoying a stroll through the park when you spot a dog wandering alone on the sidewalk. No owner in sight. Maybe it's a cat hiding under a parked car, meowing nervously. Your heart goes out to the animal, but what should you actually do?

Finding a lost pet is more common than most people realize. In the United States alone, approximately 10 million dogs and cats go missing every year. Many of these animals are beloved family members whose owners are desperately searching for them. Your actions in the first few minutes can make the difference between a joyful reunion and weeks of anguish for a family.

Here are five clear, actionable steps to follow when you encounter a lost pet on the street.

Step 1: Approach Safely and Calmly

Before you rush in to help, take a moment to assess the situation. A lost animal is often frightened, disoriented, and potentially dehydrated. Even the friendliest dog can react unpredictably when it's scared and far from home.

Start by observing the animal from a distance. Look for signs of fear or aggression: pinned-back ears, a tucked tail, raised hackles, growling, or hissing. If the animal seems aggressive or panicked, do not attempt to grab or corner it. Instead, keep a respectful distance and call your local animal control for assistance.

If the animal appears calm or cautiously curious, approach slowly. Lower your body slightly to appear less threatening, avoid direct eye contact, and speak in a soft, soothing voice. Let the pet come to you rather than chasing after it. Offering a small treat or piece of food can help build trust in those first critical moments.

Warning: Your Safety Comes First

Never put yourself in danger to rescue an animal near busy roads, highways, or in hazardous locations. If the animal is in a dangerous spot, call animal control immediately and stay nearby to monitor the situation until help arrives.

Step 2: Check for Identification

Once the animal is comfortable with your presence, the next step is to look for any form of identification. This is often the fastest way to reunite a pet with its owner.

Start with the obvious: check for a collar. Many collars carry ID tags with the pet's name, the owner's phone number, and sometimes the home address. Rabies tags and license tags can also be traced back to the owner through your local municipality or veterinarian.

Step 3: Secure the Animal

If you've decided it's safe to take temporary custody of the pet, your priority is keeping it safe and comfortable while you work on finding the owner.

  • Use a leash or improvised lead for dogs. A belt, scarf, or even a length of rope can serve in a pinch. Keep the dog on a short lead so it doesn't bolt into traffic.
  • For cats, use a carrier if you have one available. A cardboard box with air holes or even a large reusable shopping bag can work temporarily. Cats that are frightened may scratch or try to escape, so handle them gently but firmly.
  • Offer water, especially if the animal seems dehydrated. A collapsible bowl, a plastic container, or even a cupped hand will do. Avoid giving food until you know the animal's condition, some may have dietary restrictions or medical issues.
  • Find a safe, quiet space. If you can bring the pet home temporarily, set it up in a quiet room away from your own pets. If that's not possible, keep it in your vehicle with the windows cracked (never in extreme heat) while you make phone calls.

Step 4: Report to Local Authorities and Shelters

Even if you plan to care for the pet temporarily, it's essential to report the found animal to the right organizations. The owner may have already filed a missing pet report, and shelters maintain databases that can match found animals with lost pet listings.

Here's what to do:

  1. Call your local animal shelter and file a "found pet" report. Provide a detailed description of the animal: breed, color, size, distinguishing markings, and where you found it.
  2. Contact animal control in your area. They often have lost pet databases and can coordinate with other agencies.
  3. Visit a veterinarian to check for a microchip. Most vets and shelters have universal scanners that can read any brand of microchip. This is free and takes only seconds. A microchip is one of the most reliable ways to identify a pet's owner.
  4. Check local lost pet registries online. Many municipalities and organizations maintain searchable databases of lost and found pets.
Tip: Take the Pet to Any Vet for a Free Microchip Scan

Most veterinary clinics will scan a found pet for a microchip at no charge. The scan is painless and instant. If the pet is chipped, the vet can access the owner's contact information through the chip registry.

Step 5: Use Social Media and Apps

In today's connected world, social media is one of the most powerful tools for reuniting lost pets with their owners. A single post shared within a local community can reach thousands of people in a matter of hours.

  • Take clear, well-lit photos of the pet from multiple angles. Include close-ups of any distinctive markings, collar, or tags.
  • Post on local community groups on Facebook, Nextdoor, and neighborhood apps. Include the location where you found the pet, the time, and a description.
  • Use dedicated lost pet platforms such as Pawboost, Finding Rover, or your country's equivalent.
  • Share the exact location where you found the animal. Owners often retrace their pet's likely path, so knowing the precise spot helps enormously.
  • Put up physical flyers in the neighborhood where you found the pet. Not everyone is active on social media, and many owners will be searching on foot.
Warning: Protect Yourself from Scammers

Unfortunately, some people may falsely claim to be the owner. Ask anyone who contacts you to describe the pet in detail, provide veterinary records, or show photos of themselves with the animal before handing it over.

Prevention: Why Every Pet Should Be Identifiable

The reality is that many lost pets never make it home because they carry no identification at all. No collar, no tag, no microchip. Every pet owner can dramatically increase the odds of a happy reunion by taking a few simple precautions.

This information is accessible to anyone with a smartphone, making it incredibly easy for a Good Samaritan to take the right action.

Finding a lost pet on the street is a moment that calls for quick thinking and compassion. By following these five steps, you can give that animal the best possible chance of getting back to the family that loves it.
